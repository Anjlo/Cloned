Introduction

This software works with Bluetooth digital scales. It is compatible with Bluetooth body fat scale, Bluetooth bathroom scale, Bluetooth baby scale and Bluetooth nutrition scale, and stores the data collecting from digital scale into different user groups, then shows, stores and shares the data by chart and table list.

Supported Android Devices

BT Bluetooth Scales(Please consult the seller)
OS request: Bluetooth 2.1 or above
System request: Android 2.3.7 or above

BLE Bluetooth Scales(Please consult the seller)
Device request: Bluetooth 4.0 or above
System request: Android 4.3 or above

Data Stored in iWellness

Measurement data:

Received from scales, includes Weight, Body Fat, Visceral Fat, Bone Mass, Muscle Mass, Body Water, BMR, BMI (Entered into User’s Height, when got measured weight, the BMI will be calculated out automatically by iWellness)

User Profile:

A User Profile can be entered manually or uploaded from the scales (with buttons). Open and run app. Select scale type and then enter your profiles to start your first measuring. New User group can be added manually.

Receiving Data

The app and the monitor or scale  are communicated via Bluetooth. To run your Bluetooth on your device, follow the instructions below:

First run the APP, enable Bluetooth is on when your device shows request instructions automatically.

Or you can go to “Settings”. Find “Bluetooth” column and switch Bluetooth on to receive measurement data:

Below steps are needed before measurement:

  1. Tap the iWellness icon to start the App
  2. Turn on Bluetooth function
  3. Step on the scale stably to finish measurement
  4. Make sure that the scale type you choose is right.

Body Fat Scale:

  1. Ensure entered the right User Profile (Gender, Level, Height and Age) before measurement.
  2. TStep on scale with bare feet after setting. Then wait for collecting data.
  3. Leave the scale when received the data.

Bathroom Scales:

  1. This type of scale can only sending Weight data

Browsing Measurement History

  1. The User Profiles associated with the selected scale type will be listed in the upper part of the Home Page
  2. One User Profile is shown at a time. Swipe left or right to view the previous or the next profile.
  3. Tap the weight information in the middle of the screen to open the profile for the user.
  4. A series of measurement types will be displayed on Home Page. As you click that   you can see Weight, Body fat, Bone mass, Muscle mass, Body water, BMR and BMI.
  5. Tap the data for each measurement type on Home Page to see line chart detail of all measurements for that specific type.
  6. Swipe left or right to see current or previous measurements.
  7. Two-finger pinch to zoom in or zoom out on the line chart. Zoom in to see the measurements hourly and zoom out to see the measurements by year.
  8. Tap a plot point on the chart to select the measurement date, and then tap the Details button to see details of that measurement.
  9. To view the details of that plot point, tap the Details button at the top of the screen. To delete data on that plot point, tap Delete at the bottom of the screen.
  10. Swipe left or right to see the details of current or previous measurements.
  11.  Tap the arrow to the right of each measurement row to go back to the Graph view for that item.
  12. To delete data for that particular detail, tap Delete at the bottom of the screen. To delete all historical measurements for the current user, tap Delete All at the bottom of the screen.

User Management

To modify a user profile:

  1. Home Page----Settings---Scale.
  2. Tap the weight value on the main page to view history details.
  3. Tap the Settings button at the bottom of the screen and the settings screen will be displayed
  4. On the main page, tap Delete to remove present User measurement;
  5. On the Graph or List page (by tapping weight value or other Measurement value to enter into these 2 pages), Tap Delete to remove present User Measurement; Tap Delete All to remove all Data for this User. 
  6. If you want to delete a user profile: Home Page---User Icon----Edit—Delete.

Switching Display Units

    The unit display of Bluetooth body fat scale, Bluetooth bathroom scale, Bluetooth baby scale can switch among KG、LB、st; The unit display of Bluetooth nutrition scale can switch among G、ML、lb、fl:OZ. Bluetooth nutrition scale: switch unit by clicking the “Unit” on the main interface; Bluetooth body fat scale: switch unit by clicking the ”Setting” on the main interface-”My information”-”Unit”, and it can control the Bluetooth body fat scale unit switch by APP; Bluetooth bathroom scale and Bluetooth baby scale: switch APP unit by clicking the ”Setting” on the main interface-”My information”-”Unit”

Trouble-shooting and FAQ

  1. Question: Why some functions cannot be achieved on other interface?

    2. Some functions are for Specific Bluetooth Scales Using only, they can be activated when operated with right devices.

  2. Question: Why I cannot receive data from the scale?

    3. Answer: Make sure Bluetooth is enabled on your Android device. Also be sure no other iOS or Android devices are running the same App with Bluetooth turned on.

  3. Question: When I measure on a body composition monitor, the display on the monitor reads “Err2”

    4. Answer: Contacting with the feet is poor. Make sure contact subject is barefoot and feet are not too dry or too wet.

  4. Question: Can I enter measurements manually on iWellness？

    5. Answer: No. you cannot. Measurements will be only uploaded from the body composition monitor or scale.

  5. Question:Why cannot find my saved data？

    6. Answer:Do not delete related data in SD card when clear Mobile Storage.

  6. Question:Why should we disconnect other Bluetooth Devices when we are using Bluetooth Scales?

    7. Answer:Bluetooth Connection are funtioning between two single devices after been paired. Bluetooth Devices will interfare the signals together.

  7. Question:What are the criteria for judging the level of exercise?

    8. Ordinary people: people who generally do not like sports a week less than or equal to two days of exercise.

    Amateur athletes: like sports but who seldom exercise a week is less than or equal to five days of professional exercise five days.

    Professional athletes: in professional sports people who exercise, seven days a week during almost all professional people who exercise.
